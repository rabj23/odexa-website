How an engagement runs

Five stages, one continuous handoff.

Not every project needs all five — a support retainer might start at stage 4. But this is the order work moves through when we run it end to end.

01 — Discover

Requirement gathering & analysis

Workshops with the people who'll actually use the system, a review of current tools and data, and a written gap-fit analysis against standard Odoo before any custom work is proposed.

02 — Scope

Solution design & proposal

A concrete scope: which modules, what's configured vs. custom-built, which systems need to integrate, and what the environment (cloud or on-premise) needs to look like — with effort and timeline attached.

03 — Build

Configuration & development

Module setup, custom development, integrations and any AI components are built against the agreed scope, with staged environments so nothing hits production untested.

04 — Launch

Migration, training & go-live

Data migration, user training, and a go-live plan with a rollback path — go-live is treated as an operational event, not a handover email.

05 — Operate

Support, hosting & iteration

Server monitoring, patching and backups, plus a standing channel for fixes, small enhancements and the next round of requirements as the business changes.

Engagement models

How we're brought in.

Fixed-scope project

A defined implementation, migration or integration with a fixed scope, timeline and cost — best when requirements are already clear.

  • Scoped deliverables
  • Milestone-based delivery
  • Fixed or capped pricing

Managed support retainer

Ongoing hosting, monitoring and a fixed monthly allowance of development hours for fixes, tweaks and small features.

  • Server & uptime monitoring
  • Monthly hours pool
  • Priority response times

Embedded / staff augmentation

A consultant or developer working inside your team on a rolling basis — for businesses running large or long-lived system programs.

  • Dedicated capacity
  • Works inside your workflows
  • Scales up or down monthly

Our story

How this actually started.

It didn't start with a business plan. Raj was working at an Odoo implementation agency when he met Gor — and ended up working directly with him on his own company's Odoo setup, outside the agency relationship. Gor brought the business side: sales, growth, understanding what clients actually needed before a single module got configured. Raj brought the technical depth. What began as a working relationship grew into a real partnership, and over time, into something closer to family.

Odexa is what came out of that — Raj on technology, Gor on business and growth, and by now a small team of five to six working across live projects. Twenty-five-plus engagements later, across Odoo, Shopify, Oracle ERP and AI, it's still run the way it started: hands-on, not handed off.

In practice

What "end-to-end" actually covers.

The same team stays involved from the first workshop through years of running the system — nothing gets handed off between separate vendors.

Before the build

Requirement workshops, current-state analysis, and a written gap-fit assessment against standard Odoo — so the scope is based on how the business actually runs.

During the build

Configuration, custom development, integrations and infrastructure set up in parallel, tested in staged environments before anything reaches production.

After go-live

Server monitoring, backups, upgrades and a standing support channel — the team that built the system is the same one keeping it running.
